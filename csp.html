PEOPLE'S OPINION ON GENERIC MEDICENE IN AN AREA

Abstract

This study explores the opinions of individuals regarding generic medicines within Mulapet, SPSR Nellore District . Generic medicines, often considered cost-effective alternatives to brand-name drugs, are designed to be therapeutically equivalent. Despite their benefits, public perception varies significantly. Through a comprehensive survey featuring 10 yes or no questions, we gathered data on familiarity, usage, perceived effectiveness, safety concerns, and trust in generic medicines. Additional factors examined include the influence of healthcare provider recommendations, the role of medication pricing, and the availability of information about generics. However, concerns about safety, quality, and regulatory standards persist. This study aims to provide insights that could guide healthcare policies and educational campaigns to improve the acceptance and usage of generic medicines.

DETAILS

Generic Medicene

A generic medicine is a copy of a brand-name medicine that has the same active ingredient, strength, dosage form, route of administration, and intended use. It costs less than the brand-name medicine but provides the same therapeutic effect. To ensure quality and safety, generic medicines must meet FDA standards, which involves submitting an application and undergoing a review process. If approved, the generic medicine is deemed equivalent to the brand-name medicine and can be substituted for it. This allows patients to access affordable and effective treatment options, making healthcare more accessible and affordable.

Non-Generic Medicene

Non-generic medicines, or brand-name drugs, are sold under trademarked names by the companies that develop them. They are usually more expensive due to the costs of research, development, and marketing. These drugs are protected by patents, preventing other companies from making them for a certain period. After the patent expires, cheaper generic versions can be produced. While generics have the same active ingredients, some patients may experience differences due to variations in inactive ingredients. Brand-name drugs typically have extensive clinical trial data to support their use.

Introduction

A generic medicine is a pharmaceutical drug that contains the same chemical substance as a drug that was originally protected by chemical patents. Branded medicines are the drugs which are protected by chemical patents. Generic medicines are allowed for sale after the patents on the original drugs expire. Because the active chemical substance is the same, the medical profile of generics is equivalent in performance compared to their performance at the time when they were patented drugs. A generic drug has the same active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) as the original, but it may differ in some characteristics such as the manufacturing process, formulation, excipients, color, taste, and packaging.

USING

The adjacent chart indicates whether the individuals are currently using generic medicines, with 64% not using them (NO) and 36% using them (YES). This suggests that a majority of the individuals are not currently engaging with generic medicines. Understanding the usage rate is crucial for gauging the market penetration of generic medicines and identifying barriers to their adoption. The reasons behind the non-usage could range from lack of availability, distrust in quality, side effects, or simply a lack of awareness, all of which are important areas for further investigation.

SIDE EFFECTS

The individuals were asked if they experienced any side effects from using generic medicines. The results show that 78% reported no side effects, while 18% reported experiencing side effects. An additional 4% also indicated no side effects, but due to capitalization differences, it appears as a separate category. This data is crucial for assessing the safety profile of generic medicines and understanding public perception of their side effects.
